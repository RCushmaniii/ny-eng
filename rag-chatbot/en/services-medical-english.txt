Service FAQs

Q: Is medical English coaching different from general professional English?
A: Yes. Medical English requires precise clinical terminology, empathetic patient communication, and the ability to discuss diagnoses, treatments, and outcomes clearly. We focus on the specific language patterns used in clinical settings, conferences, and medical publications.

Q: Can you help me prepare for a medical residency interview in the US?
A: Absolutely. We help physicians prepare for residency interviews, including behavioral questions, case presentations, and the communication style that US program directors look for. Many of our medical clients have secured positions at top programs.

Q: Do you help with medical publication writing in English?
A: We focus on spoken communication rather than writing, but we can help you practice presenting your research findings, discussing methodology with reviewers, and communicating your published work at international conferences.

Q: I'm a doctor in Mexico who sees English-speaking patients. Can you help?
A: Yes. We practice patient intake conversations, explaining diagnoses and treatment options, discussing risks and consent, and handling the emotional aspects of clinical communication — all in English, using scenarios from your actual practice.
