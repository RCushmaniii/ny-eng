SECTION 5 — THE METHODOLOGY (WHAT MAKES IT DIFFERENT)
================================================================

Most English teachers teach English. Robert teaches professionals how to sound like the leaders they already are — in a second language.

Three principles:
- Your curriculum, not a course catalog. Sessions are built from your real scenarios, your industry vocabulary, your upcoming pressure moments. Nothing generic. Nothing theoretical.
- Your voice, sharpened. Tactical feedback on tone, pronunciation, and the small signals that separate "fluent" from "commanding."
- Your rehearsal space. Simulate the pitch, the negotiation, or the client call before it counts.

Robert specifically targets the performance gap — the moment where you know the grammar but freeze under pressure. He treats it like athletic training: high-pressure simulation drills rewire your brain to think directly in English under stress.

Q: How long until I see results?
A: Honest answer: Robert does not guarantee results, and he is upfront about that from the start. What he can say from experience is that clients who commit to sessions AND put in serious work outside of class — watching films in English, reading business content in English, finding conversation partners — typically see real, observable improvement. Those who show up to sessions but do nothing between them see limited results. After age 16 or so, elevating your English to the next level requires several hours of immersion per week, not just one or two hours with a coach. Robert will tell you this directly because setting false expectations helps no one. What he aims for: meaningful, measurable improvement in professional communication within 3 months of consistent effort.

Q: Can you guarantee results?
A: No — and Robert will tell you that honestly rather than overpromise. Results depend on three things: session consistency, the quality of preparation between sessions, and what you do outside of coaching time (English films, reading, conversation practice in real situations). One or two hours a week with a coach alone will not produce a dramatic jump in level. The coaching accelerates and directs your development — but the work has to happen on your side too. Clients who commit fully typically see real breakthroughs. Clients who attend but don't engage outside sessions see limited gains. That's the honest version.

Q: How is this different from traditional English classes or apps like Duolingo?
A: Traditional classes teach grammar rules and vocabulary lists. Apps teach you to recognize words. Neither prepares you for a board meeting where a US VP interrupts you with a hard question. Robert focuses on real-time performance under pressure using your actual work materials. If your goal is to pass a grammar test, this is the wrong service.
