## Strategy 3: HARO / Connectively / Quoted.ai

**The play:** Respond to journalist queries as a business English expert. Every quote = a backlink.

### Setup
1. Sign up for **Connectively** (formerly HARO), **Quoted.ai**, **Help a B2B Writer**, **Terkel**
2. Set alerts for: "business English," "communication skills," "remote work communication," "interview preparation," "executive coaching," "nearshore," "Mexico business"
3. Respond within 2 hours of query (speed wins)

### Positioning for responses
- **Robert Cushman, Business English Coach and Founder of NY English Teacher**
- Lead with a specific, quotable insight (not generic advice)
- Reference your data points: cost of communication gaps, transformation metrics
- Always offer to provide additional context (builds relationship with journalist)

**Expected yield:** 2-5 backlinks/month from DA 40-80 sites. This is the single highest-ROI activity for a new site.

## Strategy 5: Original Research & Data

**The play:** Publish original data that journalists and bloggers cite. This is the long-game authority builder.

### Research Projects (Pick 1-2)

1. **"State of Business English in Mexican Companies" Survey**
   - Survey 100+ Mexican professionals about English challenges
   - Publish findings as a report with graphics
   - Pitch results to Mexican business press (Expansion, El Financiero)
   - **Link magnet level: Very high** — original data gets cited for years

2. **"Communication Skills Salary Premium" Analysis**
   - Analyze job postings for bilingual vs. non-bilingual salary differences
   - Scrape LinkedIn/Indeed data for Mexican market
   - Publish as infographic + blog post
   - **Link magnet level: High** — salary data gets shared heavily

3. **"False Cognates That Cost Careers" Reference Guide**
   - Comprehensive Spanish-English false cognate database with business context
   - Interactive page on the site (searchable)
   - **Link magnet level: High** — language teachers, ESL blogs, and universities link to reference material

## Strategy 7: Broken Link Building

**The play:** Find broken links on relevant pages and offer your content as a replacement.

### How to Execute
1. Use **Ahrefs** (free trial) or **Check My Links** (Chrome extension)
2. Search for pages linking to defunct business English sites
3. Find broken links on:
   - University ESL resource pages
   - Corporate training directories
   - HR blog roundup posts
4. Email the site owner: "Found a broken link on [page]. Our [specific resource] covers the same topic and might be a good replacement."
