# 🧩 1. JOURNALIST SOURCE PLATFORMS (highest authority links)

### 💡 What it is

Platforms that connect **journalists writing articles** with **expert sources** they can quote. A journalist posts something like:

> "Looking for experts on remote team communication challenges"

You reply with a sharp insight → they quote you → **you get a backlink from Forbes, Business Insider, Inc., etc.**

This is the single highest-ROI backlink activity. One placement on a DA 80+ site is worth more than 50 low-quality links.

**GOOD response:**
```
Subject: Re: Expert insight on remote team communication

One overlooked issue in distributed teams is language hesitation —

In our work coaching Mexican engineering teams at US companies,

mentally translating from Spanish to English in real time.

This hesitation can slow team decisions by 20-30% in high-stakes
environments. The fix isn't more English classes — it's building
"reflex English" through scenario-based drills that simulate the
exact pressure of a live meeting.

— Robert Cushman
Business English Coach & Founder, NY English Teacher
https://www.nyenglishteacher.com
```
✅ One sharp insight. Real data. Quotable. Done.

# ⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

- **Writing long HARO/Qwoted responses** — 150 words max. One sharp insight. That's it.
- **Not following up** — 80% of resource page links come from the follow-up email, not the first one. Follow up after 7 days.
- **Writing generic guest posts** — "5 Tips for Better Communication" won't get links. "Why Mexican Engineers Self-Censor in English Standups" will.
- **Pitching too many podcasts at once** — Start with 2-3, nail them, then use those appearances as credibility for bigger shows.
- **Ignoring nofollow links** — Dev.to links are nofollow but still drive real traffic and brand awareness. Don't skip them.
- **Giving up after month 1** — Backlink building compounds. Month 1 is always slow. Month 3-6 is where the hockey stick happens.
