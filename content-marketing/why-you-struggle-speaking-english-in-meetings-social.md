## LinkedIn Post

Most professionals I coach don't have a vocabulary problem.

They have a performance problem.

They understand the meeting. They follow every word. They can explain their idea perfectly — in their head.

But the moment it's their turn to speak? They freeze.

Here's why: they're building sentences in real time instead of using trained language patterns.

Confident speakers don't construct — they deploy.

Phrases like:
— "Let me jump in here for a second…"
— "From my perspective…"
— "Just to add to that…"

These aren't advanced. They're simple. But they do something critical: they buy you time and give you control.

Once you start speaking, everything becomes easier.

I wrote about the real reasons professionals freeze in English meetings — and the structured approach that fixes it.
