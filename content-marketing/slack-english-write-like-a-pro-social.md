## LinkedIn Post

Your code is solid. Your PRs are clean. But your Slack messages are silently holding you back.

I coach nearshore developers across Mexico who build production-grade software for US companies. Their technical skills are excellent. But the moment they type a message in Slack, they accidentally broadcast that English isn't their first language.

It's not grammar. It's tone.

Here's what I mean:

Junior dev: "Hello team. I would like to inform you that I have completed the work on the authentication module. It is now ready for review."

Senior dev: "Auth module is done — PR is up. Switched from session tokens to JWTs, added refresh token rotation. Happy to walk through it."

Same information. Completely different perception.

In remote work, your Slack presence IS your professional presence. Your US teammates see your messages all day — they form their impression of you there, not in your code.

I wrote a guide covering the 8 Slack situations every tech professional needs to master — from status updates and asking for help, to disagreeing professionally and building rapport in #random.

## Twitter/X Thread

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Your Slack messages reveal more about your English level than any certification.

Not grammar mistakes — those are easy to fix.

It's TONE.

Here are 8 Slack situations every nearshore developer needs to master (thread):

**Tweet 2:**
STATUS UPDATES

Junior: "Hello team. I would like to inform you that I have completed the work on the authentication module."

Senior: "Auth module is done — PR is up. Switched to JWTs, added refresh rotation. Happy to walk through it."

Lead with what's done. Link the evidence. No padding.

**Tweet 3:**
ASKING FOR HELP

Junior: "Sorry to bother you, but I have a question..."

Senior: "@maria — quick question on the caching layer. Deciding between Redis and LRU cache. Redis feels like overkill for our traffic but scales better. Your take?"

Show your thinking. No apologies.

**Tweet 4:**
FLAGGING PROBLEMS

Junior: "Hi, I have a problem. The API is not working."

Senior: "Heads up — payments API returning 500s. Started 30 min ago. Looks like a Stripe timeout. Not blocking yet. I'll keep monitoring."

Structure: what, when, what you checked, impact, your plan.
