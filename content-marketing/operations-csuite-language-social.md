## Twitter/X Thread

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Operations leaders: the C-suite doesn't promote people who report problems accurately.

They promote people who report problems as managed situations.

Here's the vocabulary swap that changes everything. A thread:

**Tweet 2:**
Replace these words immediately:

"experiencing" -> "managing"
"issues" -> "constraints"
"delays" -> "throughput pressure"
"can't" -> "currently limited"
"failing" -> "underperforming"

Same facts. Executive framing.

**Tweet 3:**
Example:

Before: "We're struggling with fulfillment speed."

After: "Fulfillment speed is being actively optimized."

"Struggling" is emotional. "Actively optimized" is process language.

The C-suite funds processes. They replace people who struggle.

**Tweet 4:**
The best one:

Before: "Everything feels reactive."

After: "Operations are transitioning from reactive to controlled execution."

You took an emotional observation and turned it into a strategic narrative. That's how VPs talk.
